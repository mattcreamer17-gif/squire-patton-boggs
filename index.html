I. The frame

Three layers, one routing decision.

The practical answer separates cleanly into three layers. Roughly sixty percent of what associates and legal operations teams spend their hours on is traditional code and database work. Matter intake, conflicts checks, time capture, document management. Another thirty percent is rule-based logic, where deterministic workflows and precedent libraries beat any language model. Checklist-driven diligence, form-based filings, precedent retrieval once the precedent is correctly tagged. The remaining ten percent is generative work. First-pass drafting, argument synthesis across a matter's full document set, regulatory horizon-scanning, cross-border comparative analysis.

Firms that put a language model where Postgres would serve better waste spend and undercut their own governance posture. Firms that identify the ten percent and route the other ninety to the right layer preserve client confidence and compress the work that should be compressed.

  • 60% Traditional code & database Intake, conflicts, time capture, DMS housekeeping
  • 30% Rule-based logic Checklist diligence, form filings, tagged precedent retrieval
  • 10% Generative First-pass drafting, cross-matter synthesis, horizon-scanning
II. Case in point

VigilOre: a compliance workflow, compressed.

160 hrs under 5 min

Eduba designed the orchestration layer for VigilOre, a compliance platform operating in a jurisdiction with heavy documentation requirements. A workflow that previously consumed more than one hundred and sixty hours of expert time per matter now resolves in under five minutes, with a human sign-off at the defensibility checkpoint. The architectural lesson transfers. The compression is not the model. The compression is the context layer that the model sees, and the routing rules that decide when the model sees it.

III. The methodology

Folder structure as agent architecture.

Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM), published to ACM TiiS, sets out the folder-structure-as-agent-architecture argument that a firm committed to iManage is well positioned to apply. Matter-centric hierarchies, practice-group precedent libraries, and privilege-scoped access already speak the language the method asks for. The paper is available openly. The implementation is a client-by-client conversation.
